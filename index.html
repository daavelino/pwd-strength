Welcome to the pwd-strength project

Please do not use real passwords here.

What makes a good password, mathematically speaking?

  1. A large entropy, ideally close to the same-size password, when considering the evaluated alphabet's entropy.
    (The same-size password entropy is the best one can achieve, consider the per-symbol alphabet's entropy.)
  2. A large password alphabet, diversified enough to ensure a large entropy.
    (The entropy doesn't really matter if its password's alphabet is small.)
  3. A minimal password length, to accomodate a diversified password alphabet.
  4. A comparative alphabet, to compare the password quality against it.
